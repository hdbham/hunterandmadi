What to Know

When should I RSVP by?

Please RSVP by July 01, 2026.

What do I wear?

If you want to show up in the right vibe, think semi-formal garden party. We're encouraging jewel tones, but black and white are totally fine too. Just a heads-up: the venue is in the woods with rocky terrain, so wear heels at your own risk!

If you have questions about attire reach out to one of us and we can help!

Is this far from Salt Lake City?

It's about a one-hour drive from Salt Lake City, plus a few miles down a dirt road. Cell reception can be spotty once you're close, but emergency calling still works. There's limited Wi-Fi available, and we'll also have walkie-talkies to help everyone stay connected.

Will food be provided?

Yes! Dinner is provided Friday night (for early guests) and Saturday night. RSVP because spots will fill up fast.

Overnight guests should bring lunches, snacks, and any breakfast items they want. We'll have firepits, limited fridge space, and plenty of room for shared food setups.

Will there be alcohol?

We will have a selection of white and red wines during reception. Feel free to bring your own beer/seltzer/wine for camping.

Liquor is not permitted at this location.

Am I allowed to smoke here?

Due to fire risk and insurance policies, smoking is not allowed on the property.

E-cigs and vapes must be kept with you at all times—please don't set them down or lose them. This site also operates as a summer camp for kids from May through August, so we want to keep the area safe and clean.

Do I have to stay the night?

Not at all! Overnight camping is optional. You're welcome to stay for the celebration and head home afterward, or stay at a nearby hotel/cabin if that's more your style.

What do I need to bring?

Even though it's September, Mill Hollow's elevation makes evenings feel more like mid-October. Pack warm layers—you'll want them once the sun goes down.

Water activities are welcome and encouraged, but the lake is cold in the evenings. Midday and early afternoon are the best times to kayak or swim.

We'll be providing dinner on Friday night and again on Saturday. Overnight guests should bring their own lunches, snacks, and breakfast items. Limited fridge space will be available.

We have several firepits available for both cooking and hanging out. If you'd like, feel free to bring a bundle of firewood to contribute.

See packing list for more details.

Will it be cold?

Most likely in the evenings. Daytime is comfortable, but nights at this elevation drop fast. Expect crisp, chilly air after sunset—hoodies, jackets, and layers are recommended.

It's around 70°F-85°F on average during daylight hours. A bit cooler early morning and evening. Averages around 50°F - 60°F.

Can I bring a date?

Please reach out to either of us by July 1 so we can coordinate any added costs. We want to be mindful of all guests, and together we can accommodate meals and seating.

What if I can't make it?

If you can't join, your words mean the world to us. Leave a note here: Send love to the couple.

Things To Do

Make a weekend of it! Here are some activities and attractions near Mill Hollow.

  • Scenic drives in the Uintas — Mirror Lake Highway offers breathtaking views and photo opportunities
  • Hiking trails — Explore beautiful trails ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes
  • Lakeside picnics — Enjoy the serene mountain lakes and natural beauty
  • Kamas & Park City — Visit charming local coffee shops, restaurants, and boutiques
  • Water activities — Kayaking, paddleboarding, and swimming (bring your gear!)
  • Stargazing — The night sky at this elevation is absolutely spectacular
  • Wildlife viewing — Keep an eye out for deer, elk, and various bird species
  • Photography — Capture stunning mountain landscapes and fall colors
